Rice (Oryza sativa) is a Kharif crop in most of India and a staple food grain. It grows best in areas with high temperature (20‚Äì35¬∞C), high humidity and assured water. Major rice-growing states include West Bengal, Uttar Pradesh, Punjab, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Tamil Nadu, Chhattisgarh and Odisha.

Rice can be grown under rainfed shallow lowland, irrigated transplanted, direct-seeded, and even SRI (System of Rice Intensification) methods. Choosing the right method depends on water availability, labour and machinery.

Best Soil Type

  • Low-lying, bunded fields ideal for puddling

  • Kharif: Nursery in June, transplanting in July
  • Rabi/summer (irrigated): Nov‚ÄìDec to Jan‚ÄìFeb
  • Harvest: Oct‚ÄìDec (Kharif) depending on duration

3Ô∏è‚É£ Soil Preparation, Nursery & Transplanting

For transplanted rice, first prepare a raised or wet nursery. Select a fertile, well-drained corner of the field, apply FYM and puddle/lightly till. Sow pre-soaked and sprouted seeds, keep the nursery moist and weed-free.

Nursery
  • Seedlings should be 20‚Äì25 days old for most varieties.
  • Root dipping with bio-fertilizers (Azospirillum, PSB) improves establishment.
  • Avoid over-aged, yellow, spindly seedlings.
Main Field Preparation
  • Plough field, apply FYM, then puddle with standing water.
  • Level field properly for uniform water depth.
  • Form strong field bunds to hold water.
Transplanting
  • Spacing: 20 √ó 15 cm (rows √ó plants).
  • Plant 2‚Äì3 seedlings per hill, not big bunches.
  • Shallow planting (2‚Äì3 cm) gives better tillering.
Direct-Seeded Rice (DSR)
  • Sowing by seed drill or drum seeder in unpuddled fields.
  • Requires good weed control but saves labour and water.

5Ô∏è‚É£ Water Management & Weed Control

Water Management
  • Maintain 2‚Äì5 cm standing water after transplanting.
  • Do not keep deep water immediately after transplanting ‚Äì shallow is better.
  • Practice Alternate Wetting and Drying (AWD) in irrigated areas to save water.
  • Drain water before harvest to ease cutting and reduce lodging.
Weed Management
  • Critical period: 15‚Äì45 days after transplanting.
  • Use conoweeder/rotary weeder between rows under SRI/line planting.
  • Manual weeding or recommended herbicides as per local guidelines.
üíß Tip: For AWD, allow water to recede until small cracks appear on the soil surface, then irrigate again ‚Äì it saves water without reducing yield.

6Ô∏è‚É£ Major Pests & Diseases ‚Äì Field Identification

Stem Borer

‚ÄúDead heart‚Äù at tillering, ‚Äúwhite ear‚Äù at heading
  • Destroy stubbles after harvest.
  • Use pheromone traps, need-based sprays as per experts.

Brown Planthopper (BPH)

Brown patches, hopper burn in patches
  • Avoid heavy nitrogen and standing deep water.
  • Use resistant varieties and IPM practices.

Blast Disease

Diamond-shaped lesions on leaves and neck
  • Use tolerant varieties and balanced NPK.
  • Need-based fungicide sprays at booting/ear emergence.

Bacterial Leaf Blight

Yellow drying of leaves from tip downward
  • Resistant varieties are best solution.
  • Avoid injury to plants and heavy N.

8Ô∏è‚É£ Farmer FAQ ‚Äì Rice Cultivation

Q1. How many seedlings per hill should I plant? ‚Ä∫

Planting 2‚Äì3 healthy seedlings per hill at correct spacing (20√ó15 cm) is ideal. Bunch planting with 8‚Äì10 seedlings causes weak tillers and poor yield.

Q2. When should I apply urea in rice? ‚Ä∫

Split nitrogen into three equal parts ‚Äì at transplanting (basal), at active tillering (25‚Äì30 days after transplanting), and at panicle initiation (45‚Äì50 days).

Q3. How can I reduce water use without reducing yield? ‚Ä∫

Use Alternate Wetting and Drying (AWD), good land levelling, timely weeding, and in some cases SRI / direct-seeded rice to save water with similar yield.

Q4. Which variety should I choose for better market price? ‚Ä∫

Fine grain varieties like BPT 5204 (Samba Masuri) and regionally preferred local fine varieties often get higher price. Check local mandi preference before sowing.
