Crop Rotation

Increase Soil Fertility & Reduce Pests with Smart Crop Rotation Cycles.

Crop rotation is the practice of growing different crops in the same field across seasons to improve soil health, control pests, and increase productivity. It ensures balanced nutrient use and long-term sustainability.

Overview

Crop rotation boosts soil fertility, breaks pest cycles, reduces disease infestation, and supports better nutrient management. It also improves soil structure and increases water retention.

Common Crop Rotation Systems

Cereal → Legume Rotation

Wheat/Maize → Gram/Soybean helps fix nitrogen & improve soil.

Rice → Pulse Rotation

Ideal for Kharif to Rabi transition; restores nutrients lost in rice.

Vegetable Rotation

Leafy → Fruit → Root vegetables reduce pest buildup.

Cotton → Groundnut Rotation

Improves nitrogen balance & prevents soil sickness.

Benefits of Crop Rotation

  • Improves soil fertility & structure
  • Controls pests & diseases naturally
  • Enhances soil nitrogen through legumes
  • Reduces soil erosion & nutrient depletion
  • Improves water infiltration & retention

Sample Rotation Plan (1-Year Cycle)

Season Recommended Crop Purpose
Kharif Rice / Maize / Cotton Primary nutrient-demanding crops
Rabi Wheat / Pulses (Gram, Lentil) Restores nitrogen, improves soil structure
Summer Vegetables / Green Manure Improves organic matter & prevents disease
