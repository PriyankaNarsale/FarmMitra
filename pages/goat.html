Goat Farming

Complete Guide to Goat Care — Breeds, Feeding, Housing & Medicine

Goats are the most profitable livestock for small farmers. This guide explains breeds, feeding, shed management, vaccinations, diseases, and growth practices.

Milk per day0.5–2 liters

Overview

Goat farming is a low-cost, high-profit enterprise. Goats grow fast, need less space, and have excellent market demand.

Popular Goat Breeds in India

Jamunapari

Top milk & meat breed; 1–2 L/day.

Beetal

High milk yield & rapid growth.

Black Bengal

Best meat quality; prolific breeder.

Barbari

Fast-growing; ideal for stall feeding.

Feeding Routine

Goats need green fodder, dry fodder & mineral-rich supplements.

CategoryFeedNotes
KidsMilk + starter + greensImportant for early growth
Adults3–5 kg greens + 1 kg dry fodderProvide minerals daily
PregnantGrains + oil cakes + green fodderEssential for fetus growth

Goat Shed Design

  • Raised platform to avoid dampness
  • Good ventilation & sunlight
  • Separate area for kids & sick goats
  • CLean water & feeding arrangement

Common Goat Diseases

PPR (Goat Plague)

Highly infectious; fever & diarrhea.

Enterotoxaemia

Sudden death; must vaccinate.

Foot Rot

Hoof infection causing lameness.

Worm Infestation

Weight loss; regular deworming needed.

Deworming Schedule

AgeDeworming

Goat Farming Tips

  • Provide salt & mineral mixture
  • Keep shed clean & dry
  • Do timely vaccinations
  • Trim hooves regularly
