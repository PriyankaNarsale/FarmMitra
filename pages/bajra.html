Bajra (Pennisetum glaucum) is a hardy cereal that tolerates drought, high temperature and low soil fertility better than most crops. It is widely grown in Rajasthan, Maharashtra, Gujarat, Uttar Pradesh, Karnataka, Haryana and semi-arid regions of India.

7Ô∏è‚É£ Harvesting, Yield & Economics

Bajra is ready for harvest when ear heads turn brownish and grains become hard (usually 75‚Äì90 days after sowing, depending on variety and climate).

  • Cut ear heads first, dry them, then thresh to avoid shattering.
  • Dry grain to 12% moisture before storage.
  • Store in clean, dry, insect-free bins or bags.
