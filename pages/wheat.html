1Ô∏è‚É£ Overview & Climate Requirement

Wheat is a Rabi (winter) crop mostly grown under irrigated conditions in the Indo-Gangetic plains and central India. Major wheat-growing states include Uttar Pradesh, Punjab, Haryana, Madhya Pradesh, Rajasthan, Bihar and Gujarat.

It prefers cool, dry weather for vegetative growth and bright sunny days with cool nights for grain filling. High temperature or hot winds during grain filling can reduce grain size and yield.

Ideal Climate
  • Temperature: 10‚Äì20¬∞C during growth.
  • 21‚Äì26¬∞C during grain filling for best yield.
  • Low rainfall during ripening, no lodging.
Soil Requirements
  • Well-drained loam to clay loam soils.
  • Soil pH: 6.0‚Äì7.5.
  • Good response to fertilizers in fertile alluvial & black soils.

3Ô∏è‚É£ Land Preparation & Sowing

Wheat needs a fine, firm seedbed with adequate moisture at sowing (pre-sowing irrigation or residual moisture after paddy).

Land Preparation
  • 1‚Äì2 deep ploughings followed by harrowing and planking.
  • Level the field well to ensure uniform irrigation.
  • Apply 8‚Äì10 tons FYM / compost before final land preparation.
Sowing Time & Method
  • Optimum sowing: Mid-Oct to mid-Nov (depending on region).
  • Row spacing: 20‚Äì22.5 cm.
  • Sowing depth: 4‚Äì5 cm using seed drill for uniform placement.
Late Sown Wheat
  • Use early maturing varieties (e.g., Lok 1, PBW type).
  • Slightly increase seed rate (up to 125 kg/ha).
After Rice Wheat
  • Laser land levelling improves water use efficiency.
  • Zero-till drill sowing saves time and fuel.

5Ô∏è‚É£ Water Management & Weed Control

Water Management
  • Total irrigations: 4‚Äì6 depending on soil and climate.
  • Critical stages: CRI (20‚Äì25 DAS), tillering, booting, flowering, grain filling.
  • Avoid waterlogging, especially at seedling stage.
Weed Management
  • Common weeds: Phalaris minor, Chenopodium, wild oats.
  • First hand weeding/hoeing at 20‚Äì25 DAS.
  • Use recommended pre-/post-emergence herbicides as per local advisory for Phalaris control.
üå± Tip: Timely irrigation at CRI stage ensures good root development and maximum tiller production, which largely decides the final yield.

6Ô∏è‚É£ Major Pests & Diseases ‚Äì Field Identification

Termites

Plants dry in patches, hollow stems
  • Apply recommended termiticide in soil or seed treatment.
  • Avoid using un-decomposed FYM and remove old stubbles.

Aphids

Sticky honeydew, curling leaves, weak spikes
  • Encourage natural predators (ladybird beetles).
  • Need-based sprays as per local plant protection advice.

Rust (Yellow/Brown)

Rusty pustules on leaves and stems
  • Use rust-resistant varieties recommended for the area.
  • Spray fungicide only if needed and advised.

Loose Smut

Spikes are black and powdery instead of grains
  • Seed treatment is key ‚Äì treat seed properly before sowing.
  • Use disease-free certified seed.

8Ô∏è‚É£ Farmer FAQ ‚Äì Wheat Cultivation

Q1. What is the best time to sow wheat? ‚Ä∫

For most irrigated areas, sowing from mid-October to mid-November is ideal. Late sowing after November reduces yield due to high temperature at grain filling.

Q2. How many irrigations are needed? ‚Ä∫

Generally 4‚Äì6 irrigations are given. The most critical is at CRI (20‚Äì25 DAS); if water is limited, give priority to CRI and grain filling stages.

Q3. How can I manage Phalaris minor (gulli danda)? ‚Ä∫

Use proper crop rotation (not wheat after wheat every year), timely sowing, and recommended herbicides at correct dose and time as per local extension advice.

Q4. What is an average wheat yield? ‚Ä∫

Under good management, farmers can obtain 40‚Äì60 quintals/ha. With precision farming and best practices, even higher yields are possible.
