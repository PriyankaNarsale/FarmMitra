Soil Management

Healthy Soil = Higher Productivity. Learn Testing, Nutrients & Conservation.

Soil is the foundation of farming. By understanding soil properties, fertility, pH levels, and sustainable practices, farmers can improve crop yield, reduce fertilizer waste, and maintain long-term soil health.

Overview

Soil management refers to the use of practices that protect soil, enhance fertility, maintain structure, and ensure sustainability. Farmers must understand soil texture, nutrients, water retention, and pH to select the right crop and fertilizer.

Types of Soil

Black Soil

Rich in clay, holds moisture, best for cotton & oilseeds.

Red Soil

Low nutrients, suitable for millets, pulses & groundnut.

Alluvial Soil

Very fertile — ideal for wheat, rice, sugarcane, vegetables.

Sandy Soil

Good drainage, suitable for groundnut, watermelon.

Soil Testing Guide

Soil testing identifies nutrient levels and helps determine fertilizer needs.

Parameter Ideal Range Importance
pH 6.5 – 7.5 Balances nutrient availability
Nitrogen 280–450 kg/ha Leaf growth & color
Phosphorus 15–25 kg/ha Root development
Potassium 120–180 kg/ha Disease resistance

Improving Soil Fertility

  • Use compost, vermicompost & green manure
  • Follow crop rotation to improve nutrient balance
  • Add biofertilizers like Rhizobium & Azotobacter
  • Maintain correct pH using lime or gypsum
  • Reduce chemical fertilizer overuse

Soil Conservation Techniques

  • Mulching to prevent moisture loss
  • Contour ploughing in hilly areas
  • Planting cover crops to avoid erosion
  • Drip irrigation to prevent runoff
  • Maintaining organic matter in soil
