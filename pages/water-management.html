Water Management

Efficient Irrigation = Better Yield. Learn Smart Water Use & Conservation.

Water management ensures optimal use of available water resources through smart irrigation, rainwater harvesting, and moisture conservation practices. Proper water planning increases yield, saves resources, and protects soil structure.

Overview

Water is a critical resource for agriculture. Efficient water management reduces wastage, improves crop health, prevents soil erosion, and reduces input cost. Modern irrigation systems help farmers deliver water directly to the root zone.

Irrigation Methods

Drip Irrigation

Water reaches directly to roots; reduces wastage by 60–70%.

Sprinkler Irrigation

Best for vegetables & pulses; uniform water distribution.

Furrow Irrigation

Used for crops like sugarcane, cotton & maize.

Flood Irrigation

Traditional method but leads to water loss & erosion.

Crop Water Requirement

Crop Water Requirement Season
Rice 1200–1500 mm Kharif
Wheat 450–650 mm Rabi
Sugarcane 1500–2500 mm Annual
Vegetables 350–600 mm Any season

Water Conservation Techniques

  • Use mulching to reduce evaporation
  • Adopt drip & sprinkler systems
  • Construct farm ponds & check dams
  • Use rainwater harvesting structures
  • Promote soil moisture conservation practices
