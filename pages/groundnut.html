1Ô∏è‚É£ Overview & Climate Requirement

Groundnut (peanut) is a major oilseed, food and fodder crop in India. It prefers a warm, moderately dry climate with well-distributed rainfall during the growing period and dry weather at harvest. Major groundnut-growing states are Gujarat, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka and Maharashtra.

Being a legume, groundnut adds nitrogen to the soil, improving fertility. The crop is mainly grown as a Kharif crop under rainfed/irrigated conditions, and as Rabi/summer crop with irrigation in some regions.

Ideal Climate
  • Temperature: 25‚Äì35¬∞C.
  • Requires 500‚Äì700 mm rainfall, well distributed.
  • Dry, clear weather is ideal during maturity & harvest.
Best Soil Type
  • Sandy loam to loam soils with good drainage.
  • Soil pH: 6.0‚Äì7.5.
  • Loose, friable soil helps easy pegging and pod development.

3Ô∏è‚É£ Soil Preparation & Sowing

Groundnut is directly sown in the main field. A well-prepared, loose and weed-free seedbed is essential for good germination and pegging.

Land Preparation
  • 1 deep ploughing followed by 2‚Äì3 harrowings for fine tilth.
  • Remove plant residues and perennial weeds.
  • Apply 8‚Äì10 tons/ha FYM or compost before last ploughing.
Sowing Time & Spacing
  • Kharif: June‚ÄìJuly with onset of monsoon.
  • Rabi (irrigated): Oct‚ÄìNov; summer: Jan‚ÄìFeb.
  • Spacing: 30‚Äì35 cm between rows and 10‚Äì12 cm between plants.
  • Sowing depth: about 4‚Äì5 cm.
Seed Handling
  • Shell pods just 1‚Äì2 weeks before sowing.
  • Avoid mechanical damage to kernels while shelling.
Intercropping Options
  • Can be intercropped with red gram, castor or millets in some regions.
  • Helps in risk reduction and better resource use.

5Ô∏è‚É£ Water Management & Weed Control

Water Management
  • Critical stages: flowering, pegging and pod development.
  • Provide 4‚Äì6 irrigations in irrigated crop, depending on soil and season.
  • Avoid heavy irrigation during pegging ‚Äì keep soil moist but not waterlogged.
  • Drip/sprinkler systems improve water use efficiency.
Weed Management
  • Critical period: first 30‚Äì40 days after sowing.
  • 2‚Äì3 hand weedings or intercultivations at 15‚Äì20 and 30‚Äì35 days.
  • Pre-emergence herbicide (as per local recommendation) may be used in heavily infested fields.
üå± Tip: Light earthing-up after 30‚Äì40 days supports pegging and improves pod development in groundnut.

6Ô∏è‚É£ Major Pests & Diseases ‚Äì Field Identification

Leaf Miner

Silvery tunnels on leaves, leaf curling
  • Encourage natural enemies; install light traps.
  • Need-based spray as per local plant protection advisory.

Aphids & Jassids

Sticky leaves, curling, sooty mould
  • Monitor crop regularly; use yellow sticky traps.
  • Use recommended bio/chemical control only if needed.

Tikka (Leaf Spot) & Rust

Brown/black leaf spots, rust pustules, defoliation
  • Use tolerant varieties and proper crop rotation.
  • Need-based fungicide sprays (as advised) during high humidity.

Collar Rot / Root Rot

Seedling wilt and plant death in patches
  • Seed treatment is very important.
  • Avoid waterlogging; follow proper soil health practices.

8Ô∏è‚É£ Farmer FAQ ‚Äì Groundnut Cultivation

Q1. What is the best time to sow groundnut? ‚Ä∫

For Kharif, sow in June‚ÄìJuly with the onset of monsoon. For Rabi/summer groundnut, sow during Oct‚ÄìNov / Jan‚ÄìFeb with assured irrigation.

Q2. How do I know when groundnut is ready to harvest? ‚Ä∫

When most leaves turn yellowish and inner side of shells shows dark network pattern, pods are mature. Dig a few plants and check pods before harvesting whole field.

Q3. Why is gypsum applied in groundnut? ‚Ä∫

Gypsum provides calcium and sulphur, which are essential for pod filling and kernel quality. It is usually applied at flowering/pegging stage.

Q4. Can groundnut improve soil fertility? ‚Ä∫

Yes. As a legume crop, groundnut fixes atmospheric nitrogen through root nodules, which helps improve soil fertility for the succeeding crop.
