1Ô∏è‚É£ Overview & Climate Requirement

Maize (corn) is the third most important cereal crop in India after rice and wheat. It is grown for human food, cattle & poultry feed, silage, green fodder and starch/ ethanol industry. Major maize-growing states are Karnataka, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Bihar, Telangana, Andhra Pradesh and Uttar Pradesh.

Maize prefers a warm, frost-free climate, deep well-drained soils and adequate moisture. It is mainly a Kharif crop under rainfed/irrigated conditions but can be grown in Rabi and summer where irrigation is available.

Ideal Climate
  • Temperature: 18‚Äì27¬∞C for germination & early growth.
  • 26‚Äì32¬∞C during vegetative and reproductive stages.
  • Rainfall: 500‚Äì800 mm, evenly distributed.
Soil Requirements
  • Well-drained loam to silty clay loam soils.
  • Soil pH: 6.0‚Äì7.5.
  • Avoid waterlogged, compact or very sandy soils.
üåû Tip: Maize is very sensitive to waterlogging ‚Äì ensure good drainage, especially in heavy rainfall areas.

3Ô∏è‚É£ Land Preparation & Sowing

Maize needs a well-prepared, fine tilth with good moisture and drainage. Avoid clods and uneven land which cause poor germination and irregular plant stand.

Land Preparation
  • 2‚Äì3 ploughings with country/tractor plough followed by harrowing.
  • Incorporate 8‚Äì10 tons/ha FYM or compost during last ploughing.
  • Form broad beds & furrows or ridges for better drainage in heavy soils.
Sowing Time & Spacing
  • Kharif: June‚ÄìJuly with onset of monsoon.
  • Rabi/Summer (irrigated): Oct‚ÄìNov / Jan‚ÄìFeb.
  • Spacing (grain maize): 60 √ó 20 cm (rainfed) or 75 √ó 20‚Äì25 cm (irrigated).
  • Depth: 4‚Äì5 cm using seed drill or dibbling behind plough.
Fodder / Silage Maize
  • Closer spacing: 45 √ó 15‚Äì20 cm for higher biomass.
  • Higher seed rate: 30‚Äì40 kg/ha.
After Rice/Soybean
  • Zero-till planters can be used to save time & moisture.
  • Laser levelling improves irrigation uniformity.

5Ô∏è‚É£ Water Management & Weed Control

Water Management
  • Critical stages: knee-high, tasseling, silking and grain filling.
  • Provide 4‚Äì6 irrigations in irrigated crop depending on soil & climate.
  • Avoid stress at tasseling‚Äìsilking stage ‚Äì it severely reduces grain set.
  • Use furrow or drip irrigation to save water and reduce lodging.
Weed Management
  • Critical period: 20‚Äì45 days after sowing.
  • One pre-emergence herbicide (e.g. Atrazine as per local recommendation) + 1‚Äì2 hand weedings/hoeings.
  • Inter-cultivation and earthing up at 30‚Äì35 DAS helps weed control & root anchorage.
üå± Tip: Earthing-up not only controls weeds but also prevents lodging and improves root spread in maize.

6Ô∏è‚É£ Major Pests & Diseases ‚Äì Field Identification

Fall Armyworm

Window-like leaf feeding, frass in whorl
  • Regularly inspect whorl in early crop stages.
  • Use pheromone traps, neem-based sprays & recommended biocontrols/chemicals as per advisory.

Stem Borer

Dead-heart in young plants, bore holes on stems
  • Destroy stubbles after harvest.
  • Use light/pheromone traps and need-based control measures.

Turcicum Leaf Blight

Elongated greyish lesions on leaves
  • Use tolerant hybrids and avoid dense sowing & excess N.
  • Need-based fungicide sprays if disease spreads.

Downy Mildew / Rust

Whitish downy growth, yellowing or rust pustules
  • Use resistant hybrids and crop rotation.
  • Destroy infected plants if incidence is high.

8Ô∏è‚É£ Harvesting & Yield

Maize is ready for grain harvest when husks turn dry & brown, grains become hard and contain about 20% moisture. Delayed harvest can lead to lodging, grain damage by birds and pests, and higher shattering losses.

  • Harvest cobs, dry them in sun for 7‚Äì10 days, then shell.
  • Grain moisture should be reduced to 12‚Äì13% for safe storage.
  • Average grain yield: 30‚Äì50 q/ha under normal conditions.
  • With good hybrids and management, yields above 60 q/ha are achievable.

9Ô∏è‚É£ Farmer FAQ ‚Äì Maize Cultivation

Q1. What is the best time to sow maize? ‚Ä∫

For Kharif, sow with the onset of monsoon (June‚ÄìJuly). For Rabi/summer maize, sow in Oct‚ÄìNov or Jan‚ÄìFeb with assured irrigation. Timely sowing helps avoid moisture stress at tasseling & silking.

Q2. How many irrigations are required? ‚Ä∫

Usually 4‚Äì6 irrigations are given in irrigated maize, with priority at knee-high, tasseling, silking and grain filling stages.

Q3. How can I manage Fall Armyworm safely? ‚Ä∫

Regular field scouting, pheromone traps, destruction of infested whorls, neem-based sprays and recommended biocontrol/chemical options as per local agriculture department / KVK advisory are important.

Q4. Is maize profitable for fodder/silage? ‚Ä∫

Yes, maize gives high green fodder yield with good palatability and energy. Cutting at milk to early dough stage gives excellent silage quality for dairy animals.
