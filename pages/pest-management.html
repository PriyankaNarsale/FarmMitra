Pest Management

Identify, Control & Prevent Crop Pests using Smart IPM Techniques.

Pest management helps protect crops from insect attacks using integrated methods such as biological control, traps, resistant varieties, and safe chemical use. Early detection ensures healthier plants and higher yield.

Overview

Pest management involves monitoring, identifying, and controlling harmful insects that damage crops. Using Integrated Pest Management (IPM) methods helps reduce pesticide use and promotes sustainable farming.

Common Crop Pests

Aphids

Small sap-sucking insects; cause leaf curl and stunted growth.

Whiteflies

Spread viral diseases; found on underside of leaves.

Stem Borer

Damages rice, sugarcane; causes dead heart symptoms.

Fruit Borer

Attacks tomato, brinjal, cotton & chilli fruits.

Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

Method Description
Biological Control Use of ladybirds, parasitoids, Trichoderma & NPV.
Cultural Practices Crop rotation, field sanitation, resistant varieties.
Mechanical Control Hand picking, pheromone traps, light traps.
Chemical Control Safe use of approved pesticides only when required.

Pest Prevention Tips

  • Remove weeds regularly to reduce breeding sites
  • Use yellow sticky traps for pests like whiteflies
  • Promote natural predators in the field
  • Ensure proper spacing & irrigation
  • Spray neem oil as a natural bio-pesticide
